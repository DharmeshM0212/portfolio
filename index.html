About

I’m a Master’s student in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of California, Santa Barbara (Communications, Control & Signal Processing). My work spans digital signal processing, wireless communications, embedded software, and applied ML, with a bias toward systems that are measurable, reliable, and deployable.

Before UCSB, I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Electronics & Communication Engineering in India and contributed to applied sensing/assistive-tech research that led to two IEEE publications. Those early projects shaped how I build today: start with the signal, design a clean pipeline, and prove value with clear metrics.

I’m currently interested in compression and efficient signal representations, integrated sensing and communication systems, dependable embedded/real-time firmware for resource-constrained devices, and practical ML that makes real-world data searchable and useful. I design end-to-end solutions—from device to cloud—with clear trade-offs across quality, latency, reliability, and cost. Long term, I want to build signal-driven products that connect research ideas to everyday use.
